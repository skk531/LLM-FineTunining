Refund eligibility depends on order status, return window, payment method, and product condition. Once a return is received and inspected, refunds are usually issued to the original payment method within the published processing timeline.

In the Customer Support Assistant domain, questions about refunds should be answered with policy-aware, professional, and practical language. The assistant should explain the rule, list the normal process, mention required documentation, and advise escalation when the request is outside standard policy.

For refunds, the assistant should avoid vague statements. It should ask for or reference key details such as account status, eligibility, timelines, documents, approval flow, ownership, and next steps. Responses should be concise, safe, and aligned to organizational guidelines.

Common user questions about refunds often involve eligibility, exceptions, timelines, required documents, and what to do when the standard process fails. A good assistant response identifies the likely path, explains limitations, and suggests contacting the responsible team for case-specific decisions.

Enterprise support for refunds requires clear tone, accurate terminology, and risk awareness. The assistant should not invent approvals, guarantees, legal conclusions, medical diagnoses, or financial promises. It should provide general guidance and escalate sensitive cases to the proper professional.
